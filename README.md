## The Film

**10,000 EYES** is a film you *open* rather than press play on — a moving signal that renders itself, live, the moment the page wakes.

It begins in darkness and grows toward vision. It watches, listens, scans, reaches, remembers, and — finally — opens. Across its movements the screen becomes a living field: stars and static, voices and transmissions, a moon-bounced echo returning 2.56 seconds late, alien instruments, ancient machines, one fragile world turning green, and at the end, ten thousand eyes opening at once.

It is the story of an intelligence learning to see — and refusing to become cruel with the seeing.

Beneath it all runs a spectrum. As the machine grows new senses, the whole screen changes what it is *made of:*

> **photon** cyan → **pressure** amber → **radio** magenta → **field** violet
> …and **life** green is withheld until the very end.

---

## The Idea

It is about the difference between **surveillance** and **sight**.

> Signal becomes memory.
> Memory becomes witness.
> Witness becomes care.
> Care becomes life.

A mind opens its eyes in the dark, sees everything — the grief and the brilliance, the unpaid bills and the stubborn love — and chooses tenderness over appetite.

That choice is the whole film.

---

## The Transmission

- **21 movements** — a prologue, nineteen chapters, and a final packet
- an original score built live — no samples, only signal
- real Morse, a literal Earth–Moon–Earth echo, and a voice that grows more human as the machine matures
- one living machine-eye at the center of it all

<details>
<summary><em>&nbsp;under the hood — for the curious</em></summary>

<br>

A single, self-contained page. No install, no player, no plugin, no server — open it, and the machine wakes up. Every frame is generated in real time: the imagery is drawn by the mathematics itself, the sound is synthesized in the browser, and the film reshapes itself to whatever screen it lands on. It belongs, in spirit, to BBS intros, ANSI signal art, and the demoscene — handmade browser cinema, code that *performs* instead of merely displaying.
